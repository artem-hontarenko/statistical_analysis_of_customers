Description¶

The goal of this work is to analyze the dataset of customer purchases. We will build and test some hypotheses and check the relations between different parts of the data using some statistical methods.

The dataset consists of information about the customers(e.g. age, income, education, etc.) and some details about their purchases(e.g. purchase_frequency, purchase_amount, etc.)

Source of data – https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/sanyamgoyal401/customer-purchases-behaviour-dataset

Part 1: Hypothesis testing with chi-square test¶

Let us look at the distribution of the age of the customers to gain an insight into the client base.

In [5]:
sns.histplot(df['age'])
plt.show()

As we see, the distribution of the age is similar to the normal distribution, so we are ready to build the null hyphothesis and the alternative hyphothesis.

$H_0:$ the distribution of the age of the cusomers is well modeled by normal distribution;

$H_A:$ the distribution of the age of the cusomers is not well modeled by normal distribution;

The significance level in our testing is 0.05.

#Computing the mean value, varience and standard deviation respectively
age_mean = np.mean(age)
age_var = np.var(age)
age_std = np.std(age)

To check the hyphothesis we split the data into intervals to compute Z-score for every interval. Then we compute the CDF for every Z-score.Using the CDF we compute the expected area inside each interval and the observed one. Finally, we compute Pearson's statistics and compare to the critical value for our degree of freedom and significance level.

Part 2: Linear regression model¶

The natural question that may arise is "Is there any correlation between the level of income and the amount of money a person spends on a purchase?". Let us investigate it and try to build a linear relation between the level of income and the amount of money spent on the purchases.

$H_0:$ there is no relationship between the predictor variable and the response variable;

$H_A:$ there is a relationship between the predictor variable and the response variable;

The significance level in our testing is 0.05.
